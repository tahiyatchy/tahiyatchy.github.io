The Belmont Report

Respect for Persons

Recognizing the autonomy of individuals and protecting those with diminished autonomy.

Beneficence

Maximizing benefits and minimizing harms to research participants.

Justice

Ensuring fair distribution of the benefits and burdens of research.

Conflicts of Interest

A conflict of interest exists when professional judgment concerning a primary interest may be unduly influenced by a secondary interest.

  • Financial conflicts
  • Personal relationships
  • Academic pressures
  • Institutional loyalties

IRB Regulations and Review

Institutional Review Boards ensure that research involving human subjects is conducted ethically.

Exempt Review

Minimal risk research that qualifies for exemption from full IRB review.

Expedited Review

Minimal risk research that doesn't qualify for exemption but can be reviewed by one IRB member.

Full Board Review

Research involving more than minimal risk requires review by the full IRB committee.

Data Handling and Privacy

Proper data management is essential for protecting participant confidentiality and research integrity.

  • Data encryption standards
  • Secure storage protocols
  • Data retention policies
  • De-identification procedures
